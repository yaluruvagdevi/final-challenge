The Great Gatsby

by F. Scott Fitzgerald

*The Great Gatsby* is a timeless classic that explores themes of love, wealth, and the American Dream during the roaring 1920s. Through the mysterious and extravagant life of Jay Gatsby, Fitzgerald paints a powerful portrait of longing and illusion. The writing is lyrical, the characters are deeply flawed yet fascinating, and the tragic ending leaves a lasting impression.

Fitzgerald's storytelling is both poetic and poignant. The symbolism throughout the novel, especially the green light and the eyes of Dr. T. J. Eckleburg, adds layers of meaning that make the book worth re-reading.

★★★★☆ 4/5
